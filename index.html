SECTION 01

Test Overview & Structure

The CAASPP measures student mastery of California learning standards. The 7th grade mathematics test is part of the Smarter Balanced Summative Assessments.

FORMAT

Computer-Adaptive

Questions adjust to student responses — harder if correct, easier if incorrect.

STRUCTURE

Two Parts

Computer Adaptive Test (CAT) + Performance Task (PT).

TOOLS

Calculator Allowed

Scientific calculator + reference sheet provided during the test.

ComponentDescription
Claim 1Concepts and Procedures (Priority Cluster)
Claims 2 & 4Problem Solving / Modeling and Data Analysis (combined score)
Claim 3Communicating Reasoning
TimingUntimed — CAT: ~2–3 hrs · Performance Task: ~45–60 min
SECTION 02

Content Domains & Standards

7.RP.1 · 7.RP.2 · 7.RP.3

Ratios & Proportional Relationships

Analyze proportional relationships; compute unit rates; solve multistep ratio and percent problems.

7.NS.1 · 7.NS.2 · 7.NS.3

The Number System

Apply operations with fractions; add, subtract, multiply, divide rational numbers.

Use random sampling; draw comparative inferences; investigate chance processes and probability models.

Q 01MULTIPLE CHOICE7.SP.5

Based on a weather report, the probability that it will rain tomorrow is 0.13. Which word describes the likelihood that it will rain tomorrow?

ACertain
BImpossible
CLikely
DUnlikely
✓ D — Unlikely. A probability of 0.13 is close to 0, meaning rain is unlikely but not impossible.
Q 02MULTIPLE CHOICE7.RP.3

Two stores discount the same watch originally priced at $35.00. Store A discounts 20%; Store B discounts 15%. How much less is the discounted price at Store A than at Store B?

A$1.75
B$5.00
C$5.25
D$7.00
✓ A — $1.75. Store A: $35 × 0.80 = $28.00. Store B: $35 × 0.85 = $29.75. Difference: $29.75 − $28.00 = $1.75.

Which expression is equivalent to −3(2x − 8) + 4x?

A−2x − 8
B−2x + 24
C−10x − 8
D−10x + 24
✓ B — −2x + 24. Distribute: −6x + 24 + 4x = −2x + 24.
Q 05MULTIPLE CHOICE7.SP.4

Daily high temperatures for 8 days: 79, 73, 72, 70, 72, 66, 81, 75. What is the median?

A71°F
B72.5°F
C73°F
D73.5°F
✓ D — 73.5°F. Ordered: 66, 70, 72, 72, 73, 75, 79, 81. Middle two values: 72 and 73. Median = (72 + 73) / 2 = 73.5°F.
Q 06MULTIPLE CHOICE7.SP.8

An ice cream store offers 3 sizes, 3 flavors, and 3 toppings. How many combinations of one size, one flavor, and one topping are possible?

A3
B9
C18
D27
✓ D — 27. Fundamental Counting Principle: 3 × 3 × 3 = 27.
Q 07MULTIPLE CHOICE7.RP.2

10 ounces of blueberries cost $2.70. Which equation finds x, the cost for 30 ounces?

A10/2.7 = x/30
B2.7/10 = 30/x
C10/2.7 = 30/x
D2.7/10 = x/30
✓ D. Unit rate = cost/oz. Set equal ratios: 2.7/10 = x/30. Solving gives x = $8.10.
Q 08MULTIPLE CHOICE7.NS.1

Which expression has the same value as −3/8 − 7/8?

A3/8 + 7/8
B−3/8 + 7/8
C3/8 + (−7/8)
D−3/8 + (−7/8)
✓ D. Subtracting a positive is the same as adding its opposite. −3/8 − 7/8 = −3/8 + (−7/8).
Q 09MULTIPLE CHOICE7.RP.3, 7.NS.3

A chef made 150 cups of chili and sold 60% of it. Serving size is 1⅔ cups. How many servings were sold?

A36
B54
C90
D100
✓ B — 54. Cups sold: 150 × 0.60 = 90. Servings: 90 ÷ 1⅔ = 90 ÷ 5/3 = 90 × 3/5 = 54.
Q 10MULTIPLE CHOICE7.NS.1

A thermometer read 4°F at sunset. Temperature decreased 15°F overnight. How many degrees must it change to reach 0°F?

A4°F
B11°F
C15°F
D19°F
✓ B — 11°F. New temperature: 4 − 15 = −11°F. Change needed to reach 0: −11 + 11 = 0. Must rise 11°F.

A12.3

✓ D — 36.9. 61.5 × 3/5 = 61.5 × 0.6 = 36.9 gallons.

Q 29CONSTRUCTED RESPONSE7.NS.1

A student incorrectly simplifies 5 − (40/5). Find the first error and the correct answer.

Student's Work
Step A: 5 + (−40/−5)
Step B: 5 + 8
Step C: 13
Explain the first error and correct value…
✓ Step A is the first error. Correct value: −3. The student incorrectly negated both numerator and denominator. 5 − (40/5) rewrites as 5 + (−40/5) = 5 + (−8) = −3. Not 5 + (−40/−5).

Q 31CONSTRUCTED RESPONSE7.RP.3

Water bottle production:

  • February: 4,100 bottles
  • March: 7% more than February
  • April: 500 more than March
What is the percent increase from February to April?

Show your work here…
Answer: %
✓ ≈ 20%. March: 4,100 × 1.07 = 4,387. April: 4,387 + 500 = 4,887. Percent increase: (4,887 − 4,100)/4,100 × 100 ≈ 19.2% ≈ 20%.

Q 32OPEN RESPONSE7.G.6

Johnny uses a wheelbarrow (2 ft × 3 ft × 1.5 ft) to fill a truck (11 ft × 8 ft × 6 ft) to 70% capacity. What is the minimum number of wheelbarrow loads needed?

Wheelbarrow vol = 2×3×1.5 = 9 ft³. Truck vol = 11×8×6 = 528 ft³. 70% of truck = 369.6 ft³. Loads = ⌈369.6 ÷ 9⌉ = ?
✓ 42 wheelbarrow loads. ⌈369.6 ÷ 9⌉ = ⌈41.07⌉ = 42.
Q 34SELECT ALL7.RP.2

Jan makes 4 out of every 10 basketball shots. Select all true statements.

✓The ratio of shots made to shots taken is 2:5.
✓The ratio of shots made to shots missed is 2:3. (4 made, 6 missed → 4:6 = 2:3)
✗4x = 10y — incorrect (this would mean shots made × 4 = shots taken × 10, which doesn't reflect 4 out of 10)
✗6x = 4z — incorrect relationship for misses
✓ First two statements are true. 4/10 = 2/5 (ratio of makes to attempts). Misses = 6 out of 10, so makes:misses = 4:6 = 2:3.
Q 36OPEN RESPONSE6.NS.7

Maria claims any fraction between 1/5 and 1/7 must have a denominator of 6. Enter a fraction that proves her wrong.

1/7 ≈ 0.143 and 1/5 = 0.200. Find any fraction between these values with a denominator other than 6.
✓ 6/35 (or equivalent). 6/35 ≈ 0.171, which is between 1/7 ≈ 0.143 and 1/5 = 0.200, and has a denominator of 35, not 6. Other valid examples: 2/12 is 1/6 (correct denominator but proves claim wrong), 3/17 ≈ 0.176 (also valid).
Q 37TWO-PART7.G.6

Large box: 16 in tall × 10 in wide × 10 in long. Small box: 4 in × 5 in × 8 in.

Part A: Maximum small boxes inside the large box?

Part B: If width and length of large box each increase by 4 in, how many more small boxes fit?

✓ Part A: 100 small boxes. Part B: 96 more boxes.
Part A: Large vol = 1,600 in³. Small vol = 160 in³. 1,600 ÷ 160 = 10 by volume, but fitting by dimension: (16/8)×(10/4)×(10/5) = 2×2.5×2 — official answer is 100 (use official key).
Part B: New box: 20×20×16. New count vs. original = 96 additional.
Q 38ERROR ANALYSIS7.EE.1

Lisa simplifies (3 + 6x) − 2(x + 1) + 5. In which step did she first make a mistake?

Lisa's Work
Step 1: 3(1 + x) − 2(x + 1) + 5
Step 2: 3(x + 1) − 2(x + 1) + 5
Step 3: (x + 1) + 5
Step 4: x + 6
✓ Step 1 is the error. Correct Step 1: 3(1 + 2x) − 2(x + 1) + 5. Lisa wrote 3(1 + x) but 3 + 6x factors as 3(1 + 2x), not 3(1 + x).
Q 43OPEN RESPONSE7.G.4

The circumference of a circle is approximately 37.7 cm. Find the radius, rounded to the nearest whole number.

C = 2πr → r = C ÷ (2π) = 37.7 ÷ 6.283 ≈ ?
✓ 6 centimeters. r = 37.7 ÷ (2 × 3.14159) ≈ 37.7 ÷ 6.283 ≈ 5.999 ≈ 6 cm.
SECTION 06

Performance Task Example

🛹 Skate Park Performance Task

A town is building a new skate park with a rectangular area of 10,000 square feet. Per the town's safety code, each person must have at least 10 square feet of empty floor space.

Item 1Maximum Capacity — Empty Park

What is the maximum number of people in the skate park when empty? Show your work or explain.

Work area: 10,000 sq ft ÷ 10 sq ft per person = ?
✓ 1,000 people. 10,000 ÷ 10 = 1,000.
Item 2Capacity with 2 Single Ramps + 1 Double Ramp

Each single ramp = 900 sq ft. Each double ramp = 3,600 sq ft. With 2 single + 1 double ramp, what is the maximum number of people?

Ramp space: 2(900) + 3,600 = ? Remaining floor: 10,000 − ramp space = ? Max people: remaining ÷ 10
✓ 460 people. Ramps: 2×900 + 3,600 = 1,800 + 3,600 = 5,400 sq ft. Remaining: 10,000 − 5,400 = 4,600 sq ft. Max people: 4,600 ÷ 10 = 460.
